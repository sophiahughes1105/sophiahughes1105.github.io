üèÄ NBA Data Analysis: Growth of the Three-Point Shot

Over the last few decades, the NBA has changed in a significant way: teams are shooting more three-pointers than ever before, and this completely reshaped how the game is played. What started as a relatively minor part of the game has now become a central strategy, with many teams building their offense around it. Teams now rely on the three-point shot, but it is still not clear whether this shift has led to better results. Are teams that take more threes consistently more successful? Do they win more games or make the playoffs more often? Looking at teams from different eras, it can be seen not just how much more players shoot from deep now, but how much more important that shot has become. The goal of this analysis is to answer how the rise of the three-point shot has impacted scoring, team success, and the overall strategy of the game.

üåç The Gini Coefficient and the Environment

Income inequality is a global issue that has significant economic, social, and political effects. Additionally, harmful environmental actions and practices are seen to destroy natural resources, disrupt ecosystems, and disproportionately harm vulnerable and disadvantaged populations that do not have the technology or infrastructure to adapt to climate change and global warming.

The goal of this project is to construct a model that predicts a country‚Äôs Gini coefficient based on various environmental variables through a cross-sectional analysis of data. An ordinary least squares (OLS) regression in STATA was utilized to explore the relationship between the Gini coefficient and the various independent variables. While the Gini coefficient could be used as an independent variable to explain a country‚Äôs environmental practices, this model treats it as the dependent variable, as the focus is on understanding how environmental factors contribute to income inequality. Poor environmental practices and income inequality can be thought of as a positive feedback loop: while economic disparities may influence environmental policies and environmental degradation, the consequences disproportionately affect lower-income populations and thus further widen inequality.
