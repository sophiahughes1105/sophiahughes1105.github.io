MLB Statcast Interactive Dashboard

An interactive, multi-layer Streamlit dashboard built to explore two years (2024–2025) of MLB batted ball and event outcome data from Statcast. The dashboard features a cascading filter system allowing users to drill down by batting team, pitching team, pitcher, pitch type, and event outcome, with each selection dynamically narrowing downstream options.

The application renders four interactive Plotly visualizations: a spray chart colored by exit velocity, an exit velocity distribution, a launch angle versus hit distance scatter plot, and a Sankey diagram mapping pitch types to their resulting outcomes. The back end is structured as a modular API layer handling all data loading, cleaning, filtering, and aggregation, with Streamlit caching implemented to ensure responsive performance across a dataset of hundreds of thousands of rows.

Who Will Go Pro? College Hockey Predictive Modeling

This project applies machine learning methods to develop a predictive analytics model that identifies NCAA Division I women's hockey players most likely to advance to the professional level. Using collegiate player statistics and team performance data, the model analyzes patterns associated with post-collegiate advancement and evaluates classification performance across both skaters and goalies.

Northeastern University's program serves as a strong case study due to its consistent national competitiveness and history of producing professional-level players. Through feature engineering, model training, and performance evaluation, this project demonstrates how data-driven modeling can support scouting insights, performance evaluation, and long-term player development analysis.

NBA Data Analysis: Growth of the Three-Point Shot

Over the last few decades, the NBA has changed in a significant way: teams are shooting more three-pointers than ever before, and this completely reshaped how the game is played. What started as a relatively minor part of the game has now become a central strategy, with many teams building their offense around it.

Teams now rely on the three-point shot, but it is still not clear whether this shift has led to better results. Are teams that take more threes consistently more successful? Do they win more games or make the playoffs more often? The goal of this analysis is to answer how the rise of the three-point shot has impacted scoring, team success, and the overall strategy of the game.

The Gini Coefficient and the Environment

Income inequality is a global issue that has significant economic, social, and political effects. Additionally, harmful environmental actions and practices are seen to destroy natural resources, disrupt ecosystems, and disproportionately harm vulnerable and disadvantaged populations that do not have the technology or infrastructure to adapt to climate change and global warming.

The goal of this project is to construct a model that predicts a country's Gini coefficient based on various environmental variables through a cross-sectional analysis of data. An ordinary least squares (OLS) regression in STATA was utilized to explore the relationship between the Gini coefficient and the various independent variables.
